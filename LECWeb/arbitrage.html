Complete Markets

Equivalence of Contingent Markets and Financial Markets

Definition: Complete Markets
Given the financial structure (assets and returns), if for any two arbitrary states, we can always build a portfolio to transfer income between these two states while keeping the income in the other states constant, we say that markets are complete.

When markets are complete, the equilibrium allocation (equilibrium consumption baskets) in the financial economy and in the contingent goods economy are the same.

Intuition: If we can transfer income freely across states then it is as if we have only one budget constraint as in the case of contingent goods markets.
