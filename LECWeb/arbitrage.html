Financial Assets

Remember that in our model, a financial asset $\alpha$ is represented by a list of gross-return rates $\alpha = \left(r_\alpha^{s_t}\right)$, where $s_t$ is a state that occurs in period $t$ and $r_\alpha^{s_t}$ is the gross rate of return (i.e., the price of the asset in state $s_t$ at time $t$ divided by the price of the asset when the asset was issued). Also remember that by convention, the gross return of the asset is $-1$ at the state in which the asset is issued.

Definition: Portfolio
Let's index the assets in the economy by $k=1,\ldots,K$. A portfolio $\mathbf{z}=\bigl(z^k\bigr)_{k=1}^K$ is a list of dollar amounts invested in each asset.

Complete Markets

Equivalence of Contingent Markets and Financial Markets

Definition: Complete Markets
Given the financial structure (assets and returns), if for any two arbitrary states, we can always build a portfolio to transfer income between these two states while keeping the income in the other states constant, we say that markets are complete.

When markets are complete, the equilibrium allocation (equilibrium consumption baskets) in the financial economy and in the contingent goods economy are the same.

Intuition: If we can transfer income freely across states then it is as if we have only one budget constraint as in the case of contingent goods markets.
