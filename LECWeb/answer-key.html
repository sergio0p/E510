Midterm 1: Question 5

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of perfectly divisible rations. He must choose how many rations to consume in each period and how many to save. Chuck's utility for consumption is $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Chuck's discount factors are $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. Let $k(t)$ denote the amount of rations available at the beginning of period $t$: $k(0) = 100$. Chuck Noland's problem is then to choose how much to consume in periods 0 and 1, consuming all leftover rations in the last period. You must label all your answers and answer them in order.

  1. Write Chuck Noland's utility function at period 0 as a function of $c_0$ and $c_1$.
  2. Write explicitly the first-order conditions (Recipe #0) to maximize the above utility.
  3. Assume $c_0$ has already been chosen. Chuck Noland now faces himself in period 1 and is allowed to revise his original plan. Write Chuck's utility from the point of view of period 1.
  4. Obtain the first-order condition to maximize the utility in (c).
  5. Using the same value of $c_0$ in parts (b) and (d), in which part (b or d) does Chuck have a greater incentive to consume in period 1? Why?

Question 5(a)

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of rations. Utility: $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Discount factors: $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. $k(0) = 100$. He chooses $c_0$ and $c_1$, consuming all leftover rations in the last period.

Write Chuck Noland's utility function at period 0 as a function of $c_0$ and $c_1$.

Answer

Chuck's discounted utility is $U(c_0, c_1, c_2) = u(c_0) + 0.5\, u(c_1) + 0.45\, u(c_2)$. Since in the last period he consumes all his rations, $c_2 = 100 - c_0 - c_1$, so his utility can be written as

$$u(c_0) + 0.5\, u(c_1) + 0.45\, u(100 - c_0 - c_1)$$

Replacing $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$:

$$\begin{align*} &2\sqrt{c_0} + 0.5 \cdot 2\sqrt{c_1} + 0.45 \cdot 2\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1} \\ &= 2\sqrt{c_0} + \sqrt{c_1} + 0.9\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1} \end{align*}$$

Question 5(b)

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of rations. Utility: $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Discount factors: $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. $k(0) = 100$. He chooses $c_0$ and $c_1$, consuming all leftover rations in the last period.

Write explicitly the first-order conditions (Recipe #0) to maximize the above utility.

Answer

Since $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$, we have $u'(c) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{c}}$. Substituting $c_2 = 100 - c_0 - c_1$, the first-order conditions are:

$$\begin{align*} \text{FOC}(c_0): \quad & \frac{\partial U}{\partial c_0} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{c_0}} - \frac{0.45}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \\ \\ \text{FOC}(c_1): \quad & \frac{\partial U}{\partial c_1} = \frac{0.5}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \frac{0.45}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \end{align*}$$

Question 5(c)

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of rations. Utility: $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Discount factors: $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. $k(0) = 100$. He chooses $c_0$ and $c_1$, consuming all leftover rations in the last period.

Assume $c_0$ has already been chosen. Chuck Noland now faces himself in period 1 and is allowed to revise his original plan. Write Chuck's utility from the point of view of period 1.

Answer

Discount factors shift: from period 1's point of view, the discount factors are $(1, 0.5)$. Since $c_0$ is already chosen, Chuck's utility is:

$$U_1(c_1) = u(c_1) + 0.5\, u(100 - c_0 - c_1)$$

Replacing $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$:

$$\begin{align*} U_1(c_1) &= 2\sqrt{c_1} + 0.5 \cdot 2\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1} \\ &= 2\sqrt{c_1} + \sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1} \end{align*}$$

Question 5(d)

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of rations. Utility: $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Discount factors: $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. $k(0) = 100$. He chooses $c_0$ and $c_1$, consuming all leftover rations in the last period.

Obtain the first-order condition to maximize the utility in (c).

Answer

Since $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$, we have $u'(c) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{c}}$. Substituting $c_2 = 100 - c_0 - c_1$, the first-order condition is:

$$\begin{align*} \text{FOC}(c_1): \quad & \frac{\partial U_1}{\partial c_1} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \frac{0.5}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \end{align*}$$

Question 5(e)

Chuck Noland is on a desert island for three periods ($t = 0, 1, 2$). He has 100 units of rations. Utility: $u(c) = 2\sqrt{c}$. Discount factors: $(1,\; 0.5,\; 0.45)$. $k(0) = 100$. He chooses $c_0$ and $c_1$, consuming all leftover rations in the last period.

Using the same value of $c_0$ in parts (b) and (d), in which part (b or d) does Chuck have a greater incentive to consume in period 1? Why?

Answer
$$\begin{align*} \text{FOC}(c_1 | t=0): \quad & \frac{0.5}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \frac{0.45}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \\ & \frac{1}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \frac{0.9}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \\ \\ \text{FOC}(c_1 | t=1): \quad & \frac{1}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \frac{0.5}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \end{align*}$$

$\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \tfrac{0.9}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{c_1}} - \tfrac{0.5}{\sqrt{100 - c_0 - c_1}} \gt 0$

The above line says that at the optimal consumption plan for period 1: $c_1 = c_1^*$, chosen in period 0 (i.e., $\text{FOC}(c_1 | t=0)$ holds), Chuck's marginal utility for $c_1$ is positive. Thus, Chuck has an incentive to deviate from the original plan by increasing consumption in period 1.

Quiz 5: Question 3

There are three states $\omega_1$, $\omega_2$ and $\omega_3$ with probabilities $\tfrac{1}{12}$, $\tfrac{1}{6}$ and $\tfrac{9}{12}$. Suppose the agent's information sets are $I_1 = \{\omega_1, \omega_2\}$ and $I_2 = \{\omega_3\}$. The utility function is $u(\omega_i, a) = 1 - (i - a)^2$ where $a$ is the action chosen by the agent and $i = 1, 2$ or $3$ is simply the index of the state $\omega_i$. You MUST label your answers.

  1. Compute the probability of the first state conditional on the information set $I_1$, $\Pr(\omega_1 | I_1)$. Hint: Bayes' rule.
  2. Compute the probability of the second state conditional on the information set $I_1$, $\Pr(\omega_2 | I_1)$. Hint: Bayes' rule.
  3. What is the expected utility of the agent (as a function of $a$) conditional on the information set $I_1$?

Question 3(a)

Three states $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ with probabilities $\tfrac{1}{12}, \tfrac{1}{6}, \tfrac{9}{12}$. Information sets: $I_1 = \{\omega_1, \omega_2\}$, $I_2 = \{\omega_3\}$. Utility: $u(\omega_i, a) = 1 - (i - a)^2$.

Compute $\Pr(\omega_1 | I_1)$. Hint: Bayes' rule.

Answer
$$\begin{align*} \Pr(\omega_1 | I_1) &= \frac{\Pr(\omega_1)}{\Pr(I_1)} = \frac{\Pr(\omega_1)}{\Pr(\omega_1) + \Pr(\omega_2)} \\ &= \frac{\tfrac{1}{12}}{\tfrac{1}{12} + \tfrac{1}{6}} = \frac{\tfrac{1}{12}}{\tfrac{3}{12}} = \frac{1}{3} \end{align*}$$

Question 3(b)

Three states $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ with probabilities $\tfrac{1}{12}, \tfrac{1}{6}, \tfrac{9}{12}$. Information sets: $I_1 = \{\omega_1, \omega_2\}$, $I_2 = \{\omega_3\}$. Utility: $u(\omega_i, a) = 1 - (i - a)^2$.

Compute $\Pr(\omega_2 | I_1)$. Hint: Bayes' rule.

Answer
$$\begin{align*} \Pr(\omega_2 | I_1) &= \frac{\Pr(\omega_2)}{\Pr(I_1)} = \frac{\Pr(\omega_2)}{\Pr(\omega_1) + \Pr(\omega_2)} \\ &= \frac{\tfrac{1}{6}}{\tfrac{1}{12} + \tfrac{1}{6}} = \frac{\tfrac{2}{12}}{\tfrac{3}{12}} = \frac{2}{12} \cdot \frac{12}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \end{align*}$$

Question 3(c)

Three states $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ with probabilities $\tfrac{1}{12}, \tfrac{1}{6}, \tfrac{9}{12}$. Information sets: $I_1 = \{\omega_1, \omega_2\}$, $I_2 = \{\omega_3\}$. Utility: $u(\omega_i, a) = 1 - (i - a)^2$.

What is the expected utility of the agent (as a function of $a$) conditional on the information set $I_1$?

Answer
$$\begin{align*} \E[u(\tilde{\omega}, a) | I_1] &= \frac{1}{3}\, u(\omega_1, a) + \frac{2}{3}\, u(\omega_2, a) \\ &= \frac{1}{3}\left(1 - (1 - a)^2\right) + \frac{2}{3}\left(1 - (2 - a)^2\right) \end{align*}$$

We could simplify further, but there is no need unless we plan to use this expression. It is perfectly fine to stop here on the exam.
